## Baby Boomer

**Created by:** Nathan  
**Reviewed by:** Michael  

This ‘Boomer’ persona has a clear demographic identification. It lists relevant usability points, such as confusion around websites, their functionality, and use.  

The goals are good, though they could expand on specifics, such as quickly finding products without searching through multiple pages. More sources of information could also have been included, such as Google search or consumer and retail websites.  

The income level may be somewhat unrealistic for someone who is retired. It is unclear whether this income comes from pension, savings, or investments.  

Overall, this persona gives a decent view into the online behaviour of this generation range and their shopping habits.

## Millennial

**Created by:** Marshall  
**Reviewed by:** Nathan  

The persona is well thought out but has a few issues. One example is spelling errors such as “Solow” instead of “Slow.” These should have been corrected during proofreading, but it appears that insufficient proofreading was done.  

For the sections **Goals and Motivations**, **Frustrations**, and **Challenges and Obstacles**, the points are clear and articulate the persona well. However, more points could have been included in each section.  

The sources of information and the base details are well considered. Overall, it is a good persona that only requires a few refinements.

## Gen Z

**Created by:** Michael  
**Reviewed by:** Marshall  

This persona contains a large amount of useful information describing the average Gen Z individual. In the sections **Goals and Motivations**, **Challenges and Obstacles**, and **Frustrations**, there are four well-developed pieces of information in each category.  

Each field is filled with relevant content that fits the Gen Z demographic well. However, there are some syntax errors present.  

Overall, it is a very well-developed persona.
